The Dreamer's Compass

8-fold guided meditation inspired by Thích Nhất Hạnh

 
 
INTRODUCTION TO SATI

What is Mindfulness? Many are justly sceptical to the word these days, as it has been hijacked and misused and its meaning is at best unclear by now. So, here's what it means to me and why I find it useful and recommend it.

Mindfulness, or Sati as it may be named in the East, is a concept of Zen philosophy that has become popular in the West both as self help and therapy. I started using it to minimize the risk of becoming heavily depressed again, and I think it really helped me with that. Research shows that it can be useful to psychology in many ways. I won't go into detail here, but the works of for instance Jon Kabat-Zinn and Richard J Davidson are readily available for study.

So what is it then? To me, it's about training your mind to be present. Maybe you feel connected and aware when walking in the forest or your garden, or when exercising? True, such activities can be mindful. But what about when there is no calm sea nor greenery around, and you feel stress and other turbulent emotions? Just like we can train our bodies to be healthy and stronger, we can also train our minds to be present and able to handle more.

I'm not very diligent about it, much like I don't get as much exercise as my body needs. But a little is a lot better than nothing! When I find a moment in my day, I may meditate a bit and it feels great afterwards. Like with physical exercise it will not always be entirely pleasant but as it becomes a habit you'll love it more and more.

Pure mindfulness meditation is very simple and may therefore, paradoxically, be difficult to master: Just feel your breath to anchor yourself. Don't control anything by force. When your "monkey mind" wanders off – and it will! – lead it back to the here and now, gently and compassionately. Be your own friend, and be present. Register what you're thinking and feeling, don't force it or run away from it but guide yourself back to you, to the now. If something feels unpleasant, try just "sitting through" and observing it. Keep doing that. It's that simple, and that hard.

You can practise mindfulness in many ways. Walking slowly while being fully aware is a good practice. Nature is generally a wonderful aid to grounding your mind. You can eat or drink – tea, for instance – mindfully, by doing just that and nothing else at the same time. You can sit mindfully for half an hour in a shrine, or for three minutes on the bus. You can play a music instrument mindfully – flutes are great since you use your breath to play. With experience, you can do most things in a mindful way.

I often use a self-devised guided meditation as described below. It is still about being mindful, but with some focus points. In my opinion, this makes it more beginner-friendly but still very useful. I usually go through a route of eight points which I call my "compass", and this helps me see myself and be calmly present. Read on if you're interested!

EIGHTFOLD MEDITATION – THE DREAMER'S COMPASS

    ~ With quotes from Thích Nhất Hạnh
    ~ Compiled and written by Øystein Bech Gadmar alias "DreymaR"

I like to meditate focusing on eight areas of both body and psyche using mental images (similar to eidetic kasiṇa), after initially anchoring on the breath itself. These images are inspired by the talks and writings of Zen master Thích Nhất Hạnh ('Thay') and his pupil Svein Myreng.

The Noble Eightfold Path of Buddhism is a great inspiration. It consists of 'right' ways ('sammā' in Pali) concerning
• Wisdom (view/understanding, resolve/intention),
• Ethics (speech, action/conduct, livelihood/way-of-life) and
• Concentration (effort/diligence, mindfulness/attention, concentration/meditation).

Thay says: "It's possible for us to live the Noble Eightfold Path every moment of our daily lives. That not only makes us happy, it makes people around us happy. If you practice the path, you become very pleasant, very fresh, and very compassionate."

Below, I've combined this with the eight Chinese I Ching trigrams and their associated elements which I find a fascinating little exercise. This should not at all be overinterpreted, as for me it is merely a stimulus providing intensity and life to the mental imagery and should not lead to any rigidity or limitation!

For each of the eight points I focus on the body area and my breath for as many breaths as I feel like and have time for. During the first couple of breaths, I recite two words summing up the mental imagery for myself.

   Namaste!
   Øystein, 2019-07-26


_/|\_     Breath – Opening – Smiling

Words: "Breathing – smiling"

     Breathing in, I calm body and mind.
     Breathing out, I smile.
     Dwelling in the present moment
     I know this is the only moment.

     ~TNH~ Being Peace


¦¦¦     Legs – Roots in Earth – Touching
  
Path: Right View
Words: "Roots – connected"

     Walk and touch peace every moment.
     Walk and touch happiness every moment.
     Each step brings a fresh breeze.
     Each step makes a flower bloom.
     Kiss the Earth with your feet.
     Bring the Earth your love and happiness.
     The Earth will be safe
     when we feel safe in ourselves.

     ~TNH~ Kiss The Earth


¦¦|     Hips – Mountain – Solid
  
Path: Right Resolve
Words: "Mountain – solid"

“Breathing in, I see myself as a mountain. Breathing out, I feel solid. Mountain, Solid.”

Solidity makes it possible for us to be happy. Someone who is unstable, someone who is not solid, cannot be a happy person. When you are solid, people can rely on you. When your loved one is solid, you can rely on her. So solidity is something that you can offer to the person you love, like freshness.

     ~TNH~ Pebble Meditation, 2nd pebble

Picture a tree in a storm. At the top of the tree, the small branches and leaves are swaying violently in the wind. The tree looks vulnerable, quite fragile – it seems it can break at any time. But if you look at the trunk, you will see that the tree is solid; and if you look down to its root structure, you will know that the tree is deeply and firmly rooted in the soil.

Sitting quietly, just breathing in and out, we develop strength, concentration and clarity. So sit like a mountain. No wind can blow the mountain down.

     ~TNH~ Touching Peace


¦|¦     Abdomen – Pebble in River – Resting
  
Path: Right Action
Words: "Resting – calm"

My dear friends, suppose someone is holding a pebble and throws it in the air and the pebble begins to fall down into a river. After the pebble touches the surface of the water, it allows itself to sink slowly into the river.

It will reach the bed of the river without any effort. Once the pebble is at the bottom of the river, it continues to rest. It allows the water to pass by.

     I have arrived. I am home.
     In the here. In the now.
     I am solid. I am free.
     In the ultimate I dwell.

     ~TNH~ Resting in the River


¦||     Chest – Flower in Wind – Fresh
  
Path: Right Speech
Words: "Flower – fresh"

“Breathing in, I see myself as a flower. Breathing out, I feel fresh. Flower, fresh.” Breathe three times, reciting “flower” with each in-breath and “fresh” with each out-breath.

We human beings are a kind of flower in the garden of humanity... Every one of us has our flowerness that makes us beautiful, fresh, pleasant, lovable. When we have this freshness and beauty, we have a lot to offer to other people and to the world.

During the four or five seconds of your in- and out-breath, you can do a lot to restore your flowerness - breathing, smiling, and releasing tension. Smiling is a very easy thing to do. It does not take much time and can release many muscles in your face in just one or two seconds.

     ~TNH~ Pebble Meditation, 1st pebble


|¦¦     Arms – Lightning in Field – Active
  
Path: Right Livelihood
Words: "Lightning – creating"

"The tangerine I am eating is me. The mustard greens I am planting are me. I plant with all my heart and mind. I clean this teapot with the kind of attention I would have were I giving the baby Buddha or Jesus a bath. Nothing should be treated more carefully than anything else.”

“Don’t do any task in order to get it over with. Resolve to do each job in a relaxed way, with all your attention. Enjoy and be one with your work.”

“Only this actual moment is life.”

     ~TNH~ The Miracle of Mindfulness


|¦|     Neck – Fire – Focused
  
Path: Right Effort
Words: "Fire – focused"

     I am aware of my mind
     I make my mind happy
     I concentrate my mind
     I liberate my mind

When a thought, feeling, perception, pain, or sorrow manifests itself, practice breathing in and out and recognize it for what it is. Say, "I know you. I know you are there. I am here for you," and embrace it.

We do not need to open the door and force the cold air to go outside to make the room warm. All we have to do is tend the fire. In the case of a negative mental formation, all we have to do is look after it with the warmth of the fire of our mindfulness.

Only by concentrating steadily on an object can we observe it. The object of our mind is lit up by our observation, like a performer standing in a spotlight on a stage. The object might be moving in time and space, since it is alive. But our mind is also alive, and in the state of concentration, subject and object become one.

Breathing is an object of our concentrated mind. We put all our attention on our breath, and our mind and our breath become one. That is concentration.

     ~TNH~ Breathe, You Are Alive


||¦     Face – Still Water – Reflecting
  
Path: Right Attention
Words: "Still water – clear"

“Breathing in, I see myself as still water, breathing out, I reflect things as they are. Still water, reflecting.”

If you have seen the surface of a lake that is very still, it reflects the mountains, clouds, and trees around it perfectly.

If you are tranquil and peaceful, your perceptions will be more accurate. I want to cultivate stillness and calm. Each human being should have enough calm, tranquility, and stillness to be truly happy.

     ~TNH~ Pebble Meditation, 3rd pebble


|||     Crown – Space – Free
  
Path: Right Concentration
Words: "Space – free"

“Breathing in, I see myself as space, breathing out, I feel free. Space, free.”

Space is freedom, and freedom is the foundation of true happiness. Without freedom, our happiness is not complete. Freedom from what? Freedom from fear, from craving, from anger, from despair, from our projects and worries. Breathing in, you bring a lot of space into yourself. Breathing out, you bring a lot of space to your beloved ones.

Everyone needs some space inside and around us to be truly happy. If we love someone, we should know how to offer him enough space, inside and outside. If you don’t have enough space for yourself, how can you offer space to the person you love? Therefore it’s crucial that we know how to cultivate more space for ourselves. If you have freedom and you can offer freedom, you are a true lover. You don’t imprison yourself and you don’t imprison the person you love. You have freedom in your heart, and you offer freedom to her.

     ~TNH~ Pebble Meditation, 4th pebble

Final quote

Suppose you tell your mom, “I have a gift for you.” Your mom may ask you, “Where is the gift? ” Then you point to yourself and say, “I am the gift!” You are fresh like a flower. You are solid like a mountain. You are calm, peaceful, like still water. You are free like space. That is what you want to offer to the person you love. If you do not have any freshness, solidity, peace, or freedom, you don’t have much to offer to the person you love.

     ~Thich Nhat Hanh~
